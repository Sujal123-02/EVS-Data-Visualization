# 🌫️ Air Quality & Traffic Impact Analysis Dashboard

## 📌 Project Overview

This project presents a comprehensive data analytics framework to analyze the correlation between urban traffic patterns and air quality degradation.

By visualizing over **10,000 environmental records** from Indian urban centers (2022–2023), the study identifies **Non-Attainment Cities** and assesses compliance with national environmental standards.

The core objective is to move from simple monitoring to **causal analysis**, proving the significant impact of peak-hour congestion on **PM₂.₅** and **NO₂** levels.

## 📊 Key Features & Analysis

The dashboard is built on a **Four-Pillar Analytical Strategy**:

### 1️⃣ Spatial Analysis
- GIS-based mapping of pollution hotspots across India  
- Identification of Non-Attainment Cities  
- Regional AQI clustering  

### 2️⃣ Causal Analysis
- Comparative study of **Peak vs. Non-Peak** traffic impact on AQI  
- Statistical validation of congestion-related pollution spikes  
- Correlation modeling for PM₂.₅ and NO₂  

### 3️⃣ Chemical Analysis
Breakdown of major pollutants:
- PM₂.₅  
- PM₁₀  
- NO₂  
- SO₂  
- CO  

Benchmarked against National Ambient Air Quality Standards (NAAQS).

### 4️⃣ Temporal Analysis
- Seasonal pollution trends  
- Winter inversion impact assessment  
- Diurnal (hourly) AQI pattern recognition  
- 2022 vs 2023 comparative analysis

## ⚖️ Regulatory & Legal Framework

This project maps analytical insights to Indian environmental regulations:

- **Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1981**  
  → Identification of cities requiring Graded Response Action Plans (GRAP)

- **National Clean Air Programme (NCAP)**  
  → Tracking progress toward the 20–30% particulate matter reduction target  

- **Central Pollution Control Board (CPCB)**  
  → Compliance verification with National Ambient Air Quality Standards (NAAQS)

## 🏥 Health Impact Findings

### 🔎 Critical Insight
Peak traffic hours correlate with a **40–60% spike in PM₂.₅ levels**.

### 🚨 Public Health Risk Mapping

The dashboard directly maps AQI categories to health advisories:

| AQI Category | Health Impact |
|--------------|--------------|
| Moderate | Breathing discomfort (sensitive groups) |
| Poor | Respiratory irritation |
| Very Poor | Lung function impact |
| Severe | Severe respiratory illness risk |

# 🚀 Conclusion & Recommendations

This study transitions air quality monitoring from descriptive analytics to **causal inference-driven policy insight**.

### ✅ Key Recommendations:

- Implementation of **Low Emission Zones (LEZ)**
- Deployment of **Smart Traffic Management Systems**
- Strengthened enforcement of GRAP during peak AQI periods
- Real-time congestion-linked pollution alerts

By integrating data analytics with environmental governance, this framework provides scalable, policy-relevant insights for sustainable urban planning.
